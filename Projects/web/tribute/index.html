Salvador Dalí



or, Salvador Domingo Felipe Jacinto Dalí i Domènech, Marqués de Dalí de Púbol



by: Cody Perakslis

Life

and

Works

1904
Born in Catalania, Spain nine months after the death of his older brother of the same name.
(Salvador and his parents believe him to be the reincarnation of his dead brother.)
1919
Has first public art exhibit at the Municipal Theatre in Figueres.
1921
His mother dies of breast cancer.
1922
Studies at Real Academia de Bellas Artes de San Fernando.
1926
Gets expelled from the Academy.
Meets the artist Pablo Picasso.
1929
Father throws him out of the family home after disagreements.
Moves in with his partner, Gala.
1934
Marries Gala in a civil ceremony.
Has first United States art exhibit; it's a hit.
Gets expelled from the Surrealist group for refusing to express a political stance.
1936
Becomes the cover of Time Magazine.
1938
Meets Sigmund Freud, who claims "this boy looks like a fanatic."
1940
He and Gala move to the US to escape WWII.
1948
He and Gala move back to their home in Spain.
1958
Remarries Gala in Catholic ceremony.
1982
Recieves the title of Marqués de Dalí de Púbol from King Juan Carlos.
Gala dies.
1989
Dies of heart failure.
